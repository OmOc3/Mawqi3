## Design Context

### Users
EcoPest is used by pest control field teams. Technicians use the mobile app in the field to scan bait station QR codes, create inspection reports, save drafts offline, and sync them later. Supervisors and managers use the web dashboard to review reports, manage stations, export data, and monitor operations.

### Brand Personality
Functional, trustworthy, Arabic-first. The product should feel calm, precise, and operational, with fast readability and clear station/report status as the priority.

### Aesthetic Direction
The intended direction is a restrained field-operations interface: high-information density without clutter, dark navigation surfaces where useful, crisp light content areas, muted slate foundations, and teal only for primary actions or important operational emphasis. Avoid decorative flourishes, heavy shadows, gradients, generic marketing layouts, and playful motion.

### Design Principles
- Prioritize field usability: clear status, large touch targets, readable Arabic text, and resilient offline flows.
- Keep hierarchy practical: every card, badge, and action should map to a real workflow.
- Use the EcoPest token system: slate neutrals, teal primary actions, semantic status colors, consistent radii, and restrained borders.
- Preserve RTL and bilingual support: Arabic remains the default, with LTR handling for emails, station codes, URLs, and identifiers.
- Design for trust under operational pressure: errors, sync states, and admin handoff should be explicit and recoverable.
